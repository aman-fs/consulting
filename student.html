About Students Admission

Learn how to successfully prepare for the student visa interview.

Getting admission to a college in Canada as a foreign student involves several steps. We will be handholding you in each step enumerated below. Here is a general guide to help you navigate the process:

1. Research and Choose a College:
  • We will research colleges in Canada based on your preferred program, location, and budget. We will be your guide in this.
  • We do this Considering factors like program reputation, faculty, facilities, and campus life.
    • 2. Choose a Program:
  • Will help select a program that aligns with your career goals and interests.
  • Check the admission requirements for the specific program and guide you accordingly.
    • 3. Meet Admission Requirements:
  • We will ensure you meet the general admission requirements, including academic qualifications and English language proficiency.
  • Active help in standardized English language proficiency tests such as IELTS or TOEFL as per the band requirements.
    • 4. Application Process:
  • Help in completing the college application form
  • Pay attention to application deadlines and follow up with students and family.
    • 5. Submit Documents:

    Prepare and submit the required documents, including:

  • High school transcripts and graduation certificate- provide guidance.
  • English language proficiency test scores- Active guidance to help get excellent scores.
  • Letters of recommendation (if required)-Help you understand the process of procuring them and their impact on the application.
  • Statement of purpose or personal statement- if required help can be provided.
    • 6. Application Fee:
  • Pay the application fee as required by the college. Fees vary among institutions.
    • 7. Wait for Admission Decision:
  • Once your application is complete, please wait for the college to review it and make an admission decision.
  • Some colleges may conduct interviews as part of the selection process.
    • 8. Receive Letter of Acceptance:
  • If accepted, the college will send you a letter of acceptance (LOA).
  • Review the LOA carefully, as it contains important information about your program, fees, and other details.
    • 9. Apply for a Study Permit:
  • Once you have the LOA, apply for a study permit through the Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) website. We will be taking data from you and filling out all the forms on your behalf to ensure that the process is smooth and there are no hiccups.
  • Guide and help in preparing necessary documents, including a valid passport, proof of acceptance, and proof of financial support.
    • 10. Health Insurance:
  • Ensure you have health insurance coverage for the duration of your studies. Some provinces require mandatory health insurance for international students.
    • 11. Arrival and Orientation:
  • Plan your arrival in Canada and attend the college's orientation programs.
  • Familiarize yourself with campus facilities, resources, and support services.
    • 12. Settle In and Explore:
  • Find accommodation and settle into your new environment.
  • Explore the city, meet fellow students, and use available resources.
    • 13. Comply with Study Permit Regulations:
  • Adhere to the regulations outlined in your study permit, including maintaining full-time enrolment and complying with work restrictions.
    • 14. Keep you Informed:

    Ensure that you are informed about any updates or changes to immigration regulations and college policies.
